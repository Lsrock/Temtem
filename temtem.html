Top five dark souls bosses that will make you quit the game

As told by Jonny, who spoke faster than I could type, and written by Lucy.

  • Gael
  • Friede
  • Astraea
  • My left hand guy
  • Darth Vader

Slave Knight Gael - Dark Souls III

Slave Knight Gale. The coolest. He made the top of the list because he is the last boss of all bosses. He is the last boss of all souls games. A huge call back to the inspiration of dark souls itself, Berserk. He embodies every boss fight, like everything you've learned is put to the test when you fight him. From a giant arena, to homing AOEs, to his giant sword swings, to his automatic crossbow, to even his amazing soundtrack that plays when you fight him. Aesthetically the coolest, and one of the hardest fights in Dark Souls.

Sister Friede - Dark Souls III

Do you even know who SHE is? Shes fucking amazing. She has a ornstein and smough vibes, sister frieda definitely deserves the number two spot. Alright, let me tell ya. She's a two part battle, with number one giving a boss battle of omages. An homage to pricilla, she fights similarly . Shes just really fucking cool, with her settings, her ties to the lore, shes asthectiacally pleasing, and a great gatekeeper. She's timid and cautious in part 1, and then assertive in part two.

Maiden Astraea - Demon Souls

Her fight is really cool because you have to go through this super frustrating level, that just builds up your frustration that you want are just waiting to unleash on the boss. But when you finally get to her you realize that you don't actually want to fight her, because you learn she's just a maiden that wants to do good. But you have to kill her for the sake of the story. Like during the fight, she pleads and begs you to leave her alone so she can continue to save people. She doesn’t even fight you, her guard does. Even when you kill him, she still doesn’t put up a fight. It’s like bullying someone. And the somber sad song that plays during her fight, is a reminder that even though you are the main character, you may not always be the good guy. And that's what makes her a really cool boss battle.

Artorias - Dark souls II

Lets do my boy some justice. Hes really fucking cool. Alright here it is, my mans, are you ready? Artorias of the abyss is a really cool boss battle, because throughout the game you always hear about him. But when you finally encounter him, you come across this noble night that failed to the abyss. It’s just a cool thing to see that the legend you keep hearing about isn't about him, but it's about you. You get to see like the reality that these great heroes don’t always win. Like a slap of reality. Plus he has a dog, that he used his shield to protect him when he was defeated by the abyss and broke his arm. Even after you kill artorias you still see remnants of his actions and his legacy in the future. You get the feeling that he's passing the torch on to you. And canonically hes left handed and anyone who says differently are fucking chodes.

The Burnt Ivory King - Dark Souls II

The burnt ivory king is actually a really cool fight because throughout the DLC you can free these knights that help you though the battle. Its in like a snowy area and you fight in a fire area, and with the help you the knights that you freed you fight off corrupted knights until he comes out. And its like a war, until its a 1v2 against this guy with a lightsaber and its this really cool climatic battle with a huge fire polel in the background and opera music.
